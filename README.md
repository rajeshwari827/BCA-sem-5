## Preliminary Investigation

The preliminary investigation of the proposed **Food Donation System** identified that a large quantity of edible food is wasted daily by restaurants, hotels, and other food service providers, while many NGOs, old age homes, orphanages, and needy people struggle to obtain sufficient food. The existing donation process is mostly manual and depends on phone calls or personal contacts, making it time-consuming and inefficient. To address this problem, a web-based Food Donation System is proposed that provides a centralized platform for food donors and registered charitable organizations. Donors can post details of surplus food, and verified receivers can view and accept available donations. Once a donation is accepted, it is reserved for that receiver, ensuring proper coordination and preventing duplicate claims. The system aims to reduce food wastage, simplify the donation process, and ensure that surplus food reaches those in need efficiently. The application will be developed using HTML and CSS for the frontend, Python for the backend, and MySQL as the database.
## Problem Statement
##problem Statement


A significant amount of edible food is wasted every day by restaurants, hotels, caterers, and other food service providers, while many NGOs, old age homes, orphanages, and shelters face food shortages. The current food donation process is largely manual, relying on phone calls, personal contacts, or social media, which makes communication slow, unorganized, and inefficient. There is no centralized platform to connect food donors with verified receivers in real time, resulting in delayed responses and unnecessary food wastage. Therefore, there is a need for a web-based Food Donation System that enables donors to easily post available surplus food and allows registered charitable organizations to view, accept, and collect donations efficiently, ensuring that edible food reaches the needy instead of being wasted.
## Objectives of the Project

* To develop a web-based Food Donation System that connects food donors with registered NGOs, old age homes, orphanages, and other charitable organizations.
* To reduce food wastage by providing a centralized platform for donating surplus food.
* To enable donors to quickly post details of available food for donation.
* To allow verified receivers to view and accept food donation requests in real time.
* To prevent multiple organizations from claiming the same donation by updating the donation status after acceptance.
* To maintain records of donations, users, and transaction history in a MySQL database.
* To provide an efficient, secure, and user-friendly system that simplifies the food donation process and ensures surplus food reaches needy people on time.
## Goal of the Project

The goal of the **Food Donation System** is to develop a web-based application that acts as a bridge between food donors and registered charitable organizations such as NGOs, old age homes, and orphanages. The system aims to reduce food wastage by enabling restaurants, hotels, and other food providers to donate surplus food through an organized platform. It allows verified receivers to view, accept, and collect food donations efficiently while maintaining proper records of all users and donation activities. The project seeks to ensure that edible surplus food reaches needy people in a timely, transparent, and efficient manner.
## Scope of the Project

The scope of the **Food Donation System** is to provide a web-based platform that connects food donors, such as restaurants, hotels, caterers, and event organizers, with registered NGOs, old age homes, orphanages, and other charitable organizations. The system allows donors to register, log in, and post details of surplus food available for donation. Registered receivers can view available donations, accept suitable requests, and collect the food. An administrator manages user accounts, monitors donation activities, and maintains the overall system. The project focuses on reducing food wastage through efficient coordination and maintaining accurate records of users and donations. Future enhancements may include GPS-based location tracking, online notifications, mobile application support, and integration with volunteer delivery services.
## Purpose of the Project

The purpose of the **Food Donation System** is to provide a simple, efficient, and centralized platform that connects food donors with registered charitable organizations. The system is designed to reduce the wastage of edible surplus food by enabling restaurants, hotels, caterers, and other food providers to donate food quickly and easily. It helps NGOs, old age homes, orphanages, and other registered receivers find and accept available food donations in a timely manner. The project also maintains proper records of donations and users, ensuring transparency, better coordination, and an organized food donation process.
## Gap Analysis

The existing food donation process is mostly manual and depends on phone calls, personal contacts, or social media to connect food donors with charitable organizations. This approach is time-consuming, unorganized, and often results in delayed communication, duplicate requests, and unnecessary food wastage. There is no centralized system to manage food donation requests or maintain proper records of donors and receivers. The proposed **Food Donation System** addresses these gaps by providing a web-based platform where donors can post surplus food details and registered NGOs, old age homes, and orphanages can view and accept donations in real time. The system also maintains user and donation records, updates donation status automatically, and improves coordination, making the food donation process faster, more organized, and efficient.

# Feasibility Study

The feasibility study is conducted to determine whether the proposed **Food Donation System** can be developed successfully and implemented effectively. The study indicates that the project is feasible in the following aspects:

**1. Technical Feasibility:**
The project can be developed using HTML and CSS for the frontend, Python for the backend, and MySQL for database management. These technologies are widely available, easy to use, and suitable for developing a web-based application.

**2. Economic Feasibility:**
The project requires minimal development cost as it uses open-source technologies and free development tools. Therefore, it is economical for educational and small-scale implementation.

**3. Operational Feasibility:**
The system is designed with a simple and user-friendly interface, making it easy for donors, receivers, and administrators to use without requiring extensive technical knowledge.

**4. Schedule Feasibility:**
The project can be completed within the allocated academic timeline by following a planned development process, including requirement analysis, design, implementation, testing, and documentation.
